# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

India's night shift workers — nurses, factory and warehouse staff, security guards,
drivers, hospitality, call-center and support workers, and new parents. They are usually
tired, often reading on a phone in the small hours, and skeptical of being lectured or
sold to. They want to feel understood and to get information they can actually trust and
act on.

## Product Purpose

Moonlighters is a calm, judgment-free education platform that tells night workers the
truth about life on the night shift — sleep, body, mental health, relationships, rights,
and safety — and defuses the stigma around it. Education is the MVP that earns trust and
builds an audience; community and a wellness-kit shop follow in later phases. Success in
Phase 1: a launchable, credible site that people trust enough to join the waitlist and
share.

## Brand Personality

**Clinical · Credible · Practical.** The voice is that of a knowledgeable, plain-spoken
guide who respects the reader's intelligence and time. Trust is earned through rigor —
evidence, specifics, and citations — not through warmth-by-decoration or hype. Calm and
intimate in tone, but the calm comes from authority and clarity, not from softness.
Honest about hard truths; never alarmist, never preachy.

## Anti-references

- **Loud D2C wellness** (Hims/AG1 energy): bright pastels, emoji, hype headlines,
  supplement-brand urgency. Moonlighters is the opposite of a hype funnel.
- **Generic dark SaaS**: navy/violet gradients, decorative glows, glassmorphic glowing
  cards, the hero-metric template. The current build must shed these tells specifically.
- Also avoid: clinical-hospital sterility (stock doctors, insurance-blue) and
  spiritual sleep-app vagueness (gradient blobs, soft-everything, no substance).

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence is the aesthetic.** Credibility comes from specifics — a cited statistic, a
   named protection, a real mechanism — shown plainly. Lead with substance, not mood.
2. **Earn the dark.** The nocturnal palette serves tired eyes and the audience's real
   world; it is never "dark because tech looks cool." Decoration that only signals
   "moody" gets cut.
3. **Respect the reader's time and intelligence.** No lecture, no fluff, no hype. Say the
   true thing directly.
4. **Quiet authority over warm decoration.** Restraint, precise hierarchy, and one
   deliberate accent — not glows and gradients — carry the brand.
5. **Built for a phone at 3am.** Mobile-first, high-contrast, low-friction, fast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 (large text ≥3:1), full keyboard operability, semantic
HTML, visible focus states, and a genuine `prefers-reduced-motion` path for every
animation. Contrast matters doubly for a fatigued, low-light audience.

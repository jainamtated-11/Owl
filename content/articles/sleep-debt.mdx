# Sleep Debt Isn't a Moral Failing

If you work nights, you've probably been told to "just fix your sleep." It's rarely
that simple. Your body runs on a roughly 24-hour internal clock — the circadian
rhythm — and it expects darkness to mean sleep and light to mean wake. Night work
asks you to do the opposite, every shift.

## What sleep debt really is

Sleep debt is the running gap between the sleep your body needs and the sleep it
gets. A single short night is easy to absorb. The trouble is the slow accumulation:
an hour here, ninety minutes there, week after week.

The cost shows up as slower reactions, low mood, sugar cravings, and that foggy
feeling where you read the same sentence three times. None of that means you're
weak. It means you're human, on a schedule humans didn't evolve for.

## Small moves that actually help

- **Protect a consistent sleep window.** The same 7-hour block every day beats a
  chaotic schedule, even if it's not the "ideal" time.
- **Make your room a cave.** Blackout curtains and an eye mask trick your brain into
  nighttime at noon.
- **Use light on purpose.** Bright light early in your shift, dark and dim on the
  commute home.

> You can't always pay off the debt completely. But you can stop it from compounding —
> and that's a real, achievable win.

One of the least-talked-about costs of night work isn't physical — it's social. The
birthday dinners you sleep through. The texts you answer twelve hours late. The slow
sense of drifting out of step with everyone you love.

## It's logistics, not lack of love

When people stop seeing a night worker, they sometimes read it as distance or
disinterest. Usually it's neither. It's a clock problem. Naming that out loud —
"I'm not avoiding you, I'm asleep at 2pm" — takes the sting out of it for everyone.

## Protect a little overlap

You don't need hours. You need reliable, small windows:

- A standing 20-minute call before your shift.
- One shared meal a week that everyone plans around, awake and unrushed.
- A running voice-note thread for the people you can't sync with live.

## Let people help on your clock

Friends and partners often *want* to help but default to daytime rhythms. Tell them
what actually works: a text at 4am is welcome, a phone call at 11am is not.

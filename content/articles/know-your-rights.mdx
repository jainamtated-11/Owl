Working nights is not just "the same job in the dark." In many places, it comes with
specific legal protections, precisely because it's harder on the body. Knowing they
exist is the first step to actually using them.

## Things worth checking for your job and region

- **Night-shift allowance or premium pay.** Many contracts and laws require extra
  pay for hours worked overnight.
- **Free health assessments.** Some jurisdictions entitle regular night workers to
  periodic health checks at no cost.
- **Limits on consecutive nights and total hours.** Caps exist to prevent the kind
  of fatigue that becomes a safety risk.
- **Rest breaks and minimum gaps between shifts.** You're often entitled to a
  defined break during long shifts and a minimum rest period before the next one.

## How to use this

1. Find your contract and your local labour rules, and search the exact terms above.
2. Write down what you're entitled to versus what you actually get.
3. Raise gaps calmly, in writing, with your manager or worker representative.

> Rights you don't know about can't protect you. A single afternoon of reading can
> change what you ask for.
